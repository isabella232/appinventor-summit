CALL FOR PARTICIPATION

Submissions in line with work, experience, and implementation related to App Inventor are welcome in the following categories:

  • TALKS
  • POSTERS
  • PANNELS
  • APP COMPETITION
  • SHORT VIDEO

Each submission must conform with the corresponding guidelines outlined below in order to be considered. All submission must be received no later than the 15th May 2014, 11:59 (EST). Authors will receive notifications no later than 15th of June 2014, 11:59 (EST).

Each accepted submission must have at least one participant registered to the App Inventor summit no later than 20th of June, 11:59 (EST). Failure to register by this date might result in removal of accepted submission.

All submission should sent by email with the subject line as type of submission (talks, posters, pannel, app competion, videos). All submission received after the deadline will not be considered.

TALKS

Talks present participants with the opportunities to present their research, experience, projects or implementation of their App Inventor related work. Each presentation will last no longer than 15 minutes with an extra 5 minutes specifically dedicated to questions. Authors wish to submit possible topic to be considered for talk sessions at the App-Inventor Summit must submit a 2-3 pages abstract following the talk formatting guidelines.

Authors of selected talk must submit a final version of their talk no later than July 1st. The presentation slides as well as the 3 pages abstract will be made available to all attendees.

Please download the word template or the pdf guidlines to create and submit the talk abstract. The actual presentation file should be submitted no later than 1st July 2014. Each author can design the presentation as he/she sees fit as long as it will last no longer than 15 minutes.

 

POSTERS

Posters provide an interactive forum in which authors can present their work to attendees during poster sessions. We especially encourage students to submit their work-in-progress related to App Inventor. The poster sessions will be over lunch and coffee breaks. Posters provide an opportunity to describe new work or work that is still in progress using App Inventor.

All poster submissions must have a poster overview no longer than one page. The poster overview needs to includes title, authors, and an abstract (no more than 300 words). The final version of the poster as a separate file must also be submitted. Both files must be submitted in PDF format.

All accepted posters will be displayed during a portion of the summit, and at least one author is requested to stand by their poster during each poster session and give interactive presentation to attendees.

Attendees will vote (only once) using “VoteForMe” App-Inventor app (available to be downloaded from Google Play) for their favourite poster to be awarded at the closing ceremony.

Please use the word or latex templates to create and submit the poster overview. The actual poster should be no bigger than A1 size to fit on poster boards. Authors can design their A1 poster as they see fit.

 

PANNELS

Panels present multiple perspectives on a specific topic. Each panel will have at most four panelists including a moderator. Panel submissions should include a list of the panelists, their affiliations, and a description of the overall topic, with brief position statements from each panelist.

Proposals with more than four panelists must specify the reason for the extra panellist as well as how the time will be divided so that each panelist will be able to speak.

A panel will last no longer than 1 hour including questions. We encourage each panel to allow at least 10 minutes for the audience to respond within the session time. Panel abstracts must not exceed two pages using font no smaller than 12 pt. Times New Roman.

 

APP COMPETITION

The App Competition allows users of App Inventor to showcase their apps. In order for an app to be considered for the competition:

  • The app must be built only using App Inventor
  • The app aia file needs to be submitted for testing
  • The app needs to be accompaned by a one page documenet containing the name of the author, his current geographical location, the motivation behind the app and the reason for creating the app with App Inventor

The app developer DOES NOT need to be present at the submit. However he/she must agree that we can showcase the app if necessary. There will be three prices awarded for this competition. Each winner will also receive a certificate r eflecting the winning category.

 

Short Videos

The short video submission allows users of App Inventor to tell their story, their experience in using the tool, reasons and motivations for creating apps or just showcasing their work etc.. The video needs to be NO LONGER than one minute . Selected videos from this category will be played at the start of the 2014 App Inventor Summit. All videos submitted in this category will be uploaded to an official youTube App Inventor channel. Selected videos will also be showed on the App Inventor website. Videos longer than 1 minute will be discarded.
